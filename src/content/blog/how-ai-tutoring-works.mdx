## The Science of Knowing What You Know

At the heart of any intelligent tutoring system is a deceptively simple question: **What does this learner actually know?**

This isn't as straightforward as looking at test scores. A student might score 80% on a quiz but have fundamental misconceptions hiding beneath correct answers. They might have memorized a procedure without understanding the concept.

## Bayesian Knowledge Tracing

One of the foundational techniques in AI tutoring is **Bayesian Knowledge Tracing (BKT)**. At its core, BKT maintains a probabilistic model of whether a student has "learned" a particular skill.

For each skill, the model tracks:

- **P(Know)** — The probability the student has mastered the skill
- **P(Transit)** — The probability of learning the skill during practice
- **P(Slip)** — The probability of making an error despite mastery
- **P(Guess)** — The probability of a correct answer despite not knowing

After each student response, the model updates its belief about mastery using Bayes' theorem.

## Beyond BKT: The Learner Model

Modern systems like Veyra go far beyond BKT. We maintain a **multi-dimensional learner model** that captures:

- **Knowledge state** across a graph of interconnected skills
- **Cognitive tendencies** — How do you process information? Visual? Analytical? Example-driven?
- **Motivational dynamics** — What drives you? What causes disengagement?
- **Metacognitive calibration** — How accurately do you predict your own performance?

## The Adaptive Loop

With a rich learner model, the system can make intelligent decisions:

1. **Sequencing** — Choose the next topic that maximizes learning given what the student already knows
2. **Scaffolding** — Adjust the level of support dynamically
3. **Spacing** — Schedule reviews at scientifically optimal intervals
4. **Challenge calibration** — Keep difficulty in the zone of proximal development

## Why This Matters

Traditional education treats all students the same. AI tutoring treats each student as the unique learner they are — with their own pace, their own gaps, and their own potential.

The technology is finally ready. The science is well-established. Now it's about building systems that bring this to every learner on earth.
